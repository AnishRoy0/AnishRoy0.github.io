Motivation

Computer Science is not a mandatory subject in most middle and high schools, despite its growing importance in nearly every field. As a result, many students miss out on early exposure to essential CS skills that they are likely to use in their future careers. Additionally, there is a significant shortage of qualified CS educators at the middle and high school levels due to long hours and limited compensation, making it challenging for students to access quality instruction. As a high school student passionate about computer science, and AI/ML, I want to make advanced technology education accessible to middle and high school students in my community. Over the past year, I’ve organized boot camps that helped students gain hands-on experience, build confidence, and showcase their creativity. Partnering with the library will allow me to expand this impact in an inclusive space where students of all backgrounds can learn, collaborate, and grow. I believe the library can serve as a hub for innovation and youth empowerment, and I am excited to work together to inspire the next generation of problem solvers.
